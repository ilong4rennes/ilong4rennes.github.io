Chapter 12 Economics of inequality and poverty

12.1 Inequality

Concepts

  • equity: being fair (多指道义上，思想上的公平。）

  • equality: being equal with respect to sth.（强调资源分配上的平均、公平。）

  • income: the money received by owners of fop

  • wealth: the money or assets that people own (saving deposits, stocks, houses, painting)

    Economic inequality

  • def: degree of ppl in a population differ in the ability to satisfy the economic needs; inequality in living conditions caused by monetary factors

  • sources of economic inequality

    • income
    • wealth
    • education
    • health
    • nutrition
    • gender
    • …（race)

      Wealth inequality

  • wealth inequality arises from differences in the amount wealthy people own (savings, land, houses, jewelry)

  • income and wealth inequalities affect each other, their measurement applies to each other

  • wealth inequality > income inequality

    • low and middle income: Limited growth in wages
      • more difficult to accumulate wealth
    • High income: consume smaller % of income than low-incomes
      • easier to accumulate wealth

        Income Inequality

  • income inequality arises from differences in how even income distribution is in a population

  • income: Wage, interests from saving, rent from property

    How to measure income inequality

  • presense of inequalities in income distribution

    • poorest quintile (20%) receives less than 20% of income
    • richest quintile receives more than 20% of income

      The Lorenz curve

  • lorenz curve: degree of income inequality in an economy

  • diagonal line: perfect equality in income distribution

  • mechanism: closer btw 2 lines, greater income equality

  • diagram:

    Gini coefficient

  • def: $$\frac{area\ btw\ the\ diagonal\ and \ lorentz \ curve}{total\ area\ under\ the\ diagonal}$$

  • gini coefficient = $$\frac{A}{A+B}$$

  • range: 0-1 (can also be expressed in percentage 0-100 )

    • closer to 0, greater the income equality
    • closer to 1, greater the income inequality

    • 0.4 unequal

      12.2 Poverty

  • poverty refers to an inability to satisfy minimum consumption needs.

    Absolute poverty

  • def: refers to a situation where a person or family does not have enough income to meet basic human needs.

  • measured by a poverty line (a minimally sufficient income level to sustain a family in terms of food, housing, clothing, medical needs and so on,)

  • eg. 1.9 dollar per day – world bank’s extreme poverty line

  • 3.2 dollars – WB’s poverty line for lower-middle-income countries

  • 5.5 dollars – WB’s poverty line for higher-middle-income countries
    （这些数字不用记 有个印象即可）

Relative poverty

  • def: compares the income of individuals or households in a society with median incomes(中位数）

  • perfect income equality ➡️No relative poverty

  • the more unequal distribution of income ➡️ greater degree of relative poverty

  • eg. 50% of median income

    Minimum income standards

  • refers to a method to measure overty consisting of ongoing research on what people in a population believe are the essentials for minimum acceptable standard of living that allows people to participate in society.

    • is used to create a basket of goods needed to achieve its minimum
    • eg. food, clothing, housing, childcare, cultural participation

  • the MIS reveals important infomation about:

    • the number of people living below the minimum income required to buy the essentials
    • the relative contribution of each item in the basket to households’ abilities to achieve MIS
    • how these change over time

      Composite indicators

Multidimensional Poverty Index(MPI): measures poverty in

health: child mortality, nutrition
education： years of schooling, schoool attendance
living standards: cooking fuel, sanitation, drinking water, electricity, housing, assets

  • 0~1: the higher the value, the greater poverty
  • MPI of the world bank
    income per capita electricity
    child school enrollment coverage of key health services
    adult school attainment mainourishment (child and adult)
    limited-standard drinking water incidence of crime
    limited-standard sanitation incidence of natural disaster

Difficulties in measuring poverty

  • poverty has different meanings and difficult approaches to measure
  • measure problems
    • income measures do not take wealth or savings into considerations
    • poverty measured by use of household survey
      • subjective infomation
      • do not include homeless people and people in institutions - underestimate
      • understate income from freelance work and investment - overestimate
    • urban areas have higher cost of living - exclude the poor in urban
    • poverty lines only tells how many people, not how much the poor fall below the poverty line
  • overestimation or underestimation of the national poverty line

    12.4 The impact of income and wealth inequality

economic growth (bad for income & wealth inequality: 富人越富)
standards of living
social stability

On economic growth

  • inequality reduces the ability of lower income households to invest in human and physical capital

    • ➡️lower labor productivity
    • ➡️Lower economic growth
    • income inequality ➡️ inequality of opportunities in education
      Reasons why inequality leads to lower growth:

  • inequality reduces the ability of lower income households to invest in human and physical capital

    • lower labor productivity
    • lower economic growth

  • income inequality

    • inequality of oppotunities in education
    • low income is transferred to next generation

  • the wealthy spend lower fraction of incomes, and invest more abroad

    • 👉reduced resources available for domestic investment services

  • Reduced resources available for domestic investments

  • significant political control by the rich

    • policies in favor of the rich
    • go against interests of whole population

  • significant political control by the rich

    • Less gov provision of merit goods
    • go against interests of lower income group
    • lower econ growth
      (贫富差距大弊大于利)

  • inequality decreases the demand for locally produced goods and services

    • discourage local production
    • lower employment and investment
    • lower growth

  • high inequality

    • 👉the poor do not have collateral
    • 👉unable to obtain credit
    • 👉fewer investment(eg education, health care in human capital)
    • 👉lower growth

      On living standards

  • lack of access to health care and education

  • higher infant, child and maternal mortality

  • higher levels of preventable diseases

  • social problems

  • Inability to realize one’s full potential

    On social and political stability

(如果社会阶级差距比较大 会起义混乱之类的）

  • polarize and divide societies
    • reduce social solidarity and trust in system
  • strong political influence by the rich
    • ➡️In favor of the rich
    • ➡️vicious circle of greater economic inequality and political inequality
  • dissatisfaction to the poor (feeling socially inferior)
    • ➡️social conflict (especially when the gov further serve the interests of supporters)

      12.5 policies to reduce inequalities and poverty

Direct taxes

def: taxes paid directly the government tax authorities by the taxpayer.

  • personal income taxes
    • most important source of gov tax revenue
    • on all forms of income
  • corporate income taxes
    • Taxes o the profits of corporations
  • wealth taxes
    • taxes on the ownership of assets
    • eg. Property taxes, inheritance taxes
  • social insurance contribution
    • Taxes paid by workers and their employees, used to finance specific expenditure
    • eg. pensions, social insurance

      Indirect taxes

def: taxes on spending on goods and services, paid indirectly through suppliers to the government.

  • general expenditure taxes/sales taxes

    • taxes on spending or sales of goods and services
    • Fixed % of the retail price of goods and services(就是价格的百分之多少）

  • excise taxes

    • taxes paid on specific goods and services

  • customs duties / tariffs

    • tax on imports

  • to ⬇️Imports and ⬆️ tax revenue

    Proportional, progressive, regressive taxation

  • proportional taxation

    • as income increase, the fraction of income paid as taxes remains constant
    • constant tax rate
    • eg. corporate taxes, social insurance contributions

  • progressive taxation

    • as income increase, the fraction of income paid as taxes increases

  • regressive taxation

    • as income increase, the fraction of income paid as taxes decreases
    • decreasing tax rate
    • eg. Indirect taxes(fixed amount)

  • the more progressive a tax system, the more equal is the after-tax distribution of income
    (proportional 就对于equity没有任何用）

Evaluating taxes as a policy for redistribution

Pros

  • direct taxes which are progressive improve quality
    • … and thus economic growth
  • automatic stabilizer
  • tax revenue ->use these money to fund -> help the poor
    *diagram of promoting equity: lorenz curve inward

Cons

  • personal income taxes reduce incentive to work among high income earners
    • reduce quantity of labor and savings
    • lower growth
  • corporate income taxes reduce incentive to invest
    • lower productin of new capitals
    • lower growth
  • wealth raxes reduce incentive to invest in productive capitals
    • reduce efficiency and innovation
    • lower growth

      Other policies to reduce income and wealth inequalities and poverty

Polices to reduce inequality of oppotunity

  • Investment in human capital
    • universal education and health care
      (free of charge for very low incomes)

Targeted gov spending on goods and services

  • provision of merit goods: education, health care, infrastructure

    Transfer payment

def: payments made by the gov to individuals specifically for the purpose of redistributing(transferring) income away from those who work and pay taxes and towards those who need assistance.

  • vulnerable people: older people / sick /very poor / children of poor families/ unemployed / disabled people / …

    Universal basic income

def: is a method intended to provide residents in a country with a sum of money that they would receive regardless of any other income they may have

  • funds from taxes and savings from cutting other social service programs
    pros:

  • effective in reducing poverty

  • free to leave jobs unhappy with

  • like automatic stabilizers during recession
    cons:

  • too expensive to provide to all people

  • oppotunity cost of gov spending

  • reduced incentive to work

    Policies to reduce discrimination

  • by laws, education, management training on anti-discrimination practices, communications to employees

    Gov intervention

  • minimum wage

  • price ceiling

    • make food more affordable to low-income groups
    • rent control to support low-income people

  • price floor for farmers (+ gov purchase)

    • increase incoem for farmenrs
    • both price ceiling and price floor lead to allocative inefficiency and loss of social surplus
